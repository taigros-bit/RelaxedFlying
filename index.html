The science
✈ Aircraft physics
Where you sit changes everything.
An aircraft behaves like a seesaw. The wing is the pivot point — the center of gravity. The further from the wing, the more you feel every bump. Rear seats experience 2–3× the turbulence of wing seats due to the longer lever arm and fuselage flex.

🌤 Turbulence increasing
Climate change is making flights bumpier.
Clear-air turbulence over the North Atlantic has increased 55%+ since the 1970s. Jet stream intensification means knowing where to sit matters more every year.
💡 Pro tip
Morning flights are smoother.
Air is denser and more stable in the early morning. Afternoon solar heating creates thermal turbulence. Go early whenever possible.
